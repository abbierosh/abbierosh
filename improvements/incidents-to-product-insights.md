# Product Insight: What Repeated Incidents Usually Tell Us

## Problem

Individual incidents often look unrelated on the surface.

One might be a missing event complaint. Another might be a campaign that appears delayed. Another might be a report that suddenly drops. But over time, the same themes tend to show up:

- unclear workflow state
- weak visibility into processing stages
- status fields that do not match user expectations
- reporting that looks final before the underlying data has settled

## Approach

Instead of treating each issue as standalone, I look for repeat patterns across investigations:

- which system stage is most often unclear?
- where do support teams need manual validation?
- which customer-facing symptoms keep recurring?
- which statuses or dashboards create false confidence?

Once those patterns are visible, they can feed back into product and operations planning.

## Reasoning

A single query can solve a case.

A pattern across ten cases can point to a product problem.

That is usually where Product Ops can add the most value: translating repeated operational pain into something more structured:

- a better alert
- a new status definition
- a workflow change
- a reporting caveat
- a product backlog item

## Impact

- less reactive issue handling over time
- better prioritisation of product and platform improvements
- stronger feedback loop between support, ops, and engineering
- clearer understanding of where customers are experiencing uncertainty

## Product Ops Lens

This is the part of the role I find most valuable.

The investigation is important, but the bigger win is being able to say: this is not just one odd case, this is a repeat pattern and here is what the system is telling us.
